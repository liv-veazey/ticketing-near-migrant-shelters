Decrease in OATH Summons Near Migrant Shelters

Two years of ticketing data shows that tickets for infractions like public urination, illegal vending, and traffic violations tend to decrease around the city’s large migrant shelters.

Olivya Veazey

December 20, 2024

An analysis of NYPD data found that the number of tickets given in the four police precincts with the largest emergency migrant shelters decreased in all but one precinct.

During the past two years, the number of people residing in those shelters has decreased only slightly, but the number of tickets given for minor infractions has decreased by as much as 54%.

According to Ariel G. Ruiz Soto, a Senior Policy Analyst at the Migration Policy Institute, “In fact, some studies indicate that immigration can lower criminal activity, especially violent crime, in places with inclusive policies and social environments where immigrant populations are well established.”

Despite that data, New York City Mayor Eric Adams has become increasingly outspoken about cracking down on crimes committed by migrants.
